# L3AI Platform Product Book Speaker Notes

Purpose: customer-facing product guide for explaining how the public L3AI platform experience works.

## Slide 01 - How does the L3AI platform actually work?

Use this book after the Story Book when the customer asks what the platform does.

## Slide 02 - What problem does the product solve on day one?

The product job is to make the first review path clear.

## Slide 03 - Where does a new visitor start?

The first screen gives orientation before any deeper material.

## Slide 04 - How does registration fit into the journey?

Registration is treated as an onboarding step, not a pressure point.

## Slide 05 - What is the core user flow?

A user can follow the same loop without needing private system access.

## Slide 06 - What modules make up the platform?

L3AI is easier to evaluate when the modules are shown as one system.

## Slide 07 - What does AI Quant do for the customer?

AI Quant is an explanation layer, not a guarantee layer.

## Slide 08 - How does a user read an AI Quant screen?

The screen is useful because it explains the situation before action.

## Slide 09 - What stays outside the AI Quant claim boundary?

The boundary is visible because trust is part of the product.

## Slide 10 - How does wallet-aware education work?

The wallet experience explains state without exposing private material.

## Slide 11 - What should a user understand before a wallet moment?

The product slows down the wallet moment so the user can understand it.

## Slide 12 - Where does Compute fit?

Compute gives the product system a way to organize work over time.

## Slide 13 - What automation can the platform support?

Automation should reduce confusion while keeping review gates visible.

## Slide 14 - How does membership change the product experience?

Membership gives the product memory without overstating what is live.

## Slide 15 - What role does the community play?

Community should teach before it amplifies.

## Slide 16 - How does the business model connect to the product?

A better product path makes business conversations cleaner.

## Slide 17 - How does a customer move from interest to review?

The conversion path is review-led, not pressure-led.

## Slide 18 - What is Current today?

Current means the user can open it now on the public site.

## Slide 19 - What is In Progress?

In Progress means it is being shaped, not promised as live.

## Slide 20 - What is Planned?

Planned work stays in the roadmap lane until it is ready.

## Slide 21 - How should a customer compare L3AI to ordinary content sites?

The proof is not one document. It is the connected package.

## Slide 22 - Which audience gets which path?

The same platform supports different review paths without changing the truth.

## Slide 23 - What should never appear in the public product book?

Public product material must stay safe enough to share.

## Slide 24 - What is the next step after this Product Book?

The Product Book turns curiosity into a clean review path.

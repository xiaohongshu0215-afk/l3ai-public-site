# L3AI Real Platform Product Book Speaker Notes

Purpose: customer-facing and partner-safe product guide for explaining the actual L3AI platform modules.

## Slide 01 - L3AI is the platform, not the website.

Use this version when a customer or partner asks what the actual L3AI product does.

## Slide 02 - The previous book explained the wrapper, not the product.

The website distributes the story. The product is the application system behind it.

## Slide 03 - Scenario: a visitor becomes a platform user.

Register,Dashboard,Wallet,Products,Growth

## Slide 04 - The real platform has eight connected domains.

Dashboard,Wallet,AI Quant,Compute,Sec60,Growth,Universe,Admin

## Slide 05 - Scenario: the user logs in and needs one clear home.

The dashboard is the first real product screen after authentication.

## Slide 06 - Identity is more than a login form.

Authentication, profile and security turn a visitor into a product user.

## Slide 07 - Scenario: the user asks where their assets live.

Wallet is a controlled asset-state surface, not a generic marketing page.

## Slide 08 - Wallet copy must stay inside a safe boundary.

Public material may show wallet workflows, but never private signing, keys, secrets or live user data.

## Slide 09 - Scenario: the user explores compute products.

Compute is a real product center with order, release and claim lifecycle.

## Slide 10 - Compute has a complete operational loop.

The public book should explain lifecycle and controls, not promise fixed returns.

## Slide 11 - Scenario: the user wants AI-oriented portfolio context.

AI Quant is a product workflow with state and controls, not a vague AI slogan.

## Slide 12 - AI Quant claims need strict wording.

AI is positioned as decision support and product workflow, not a guarantee layer.

## Slide 13 - Scenario: the user enters a short-duration market surface.

Sec60 is a high-risk product surface and must be explained with risk-first language.

## Slide 14 - Sec60 needs settlement and risk context.

The core story is the full order-to-settlement loop.

## Slide 15 - Scenario: the user grows inside the platform.

Growth mechanics are product state, not just promotional decoration.

## Slide 16 - Scenario: the user invites and manages a team path.

Referral and team features must be framed around eligibility, auditability and anti-abuse boundaries.

## Slide 17 - Revenue history closes the user feedback loop.

History and revenue surfaces help users understand state over time.

## Slide 18 - Scenario: the user enters the ecosystem layer.

Universe is a product ecosystem layer with current and planned surfaces.

## Slide 19 - Scenario: the user deploys or reviews AI agents.

Agents give the ecosystem a tangible product object, not just lore.

## Slide 20 - DAO, guild, market and NFT are roadmap-sensitive.

Roadmap features stay clearly marked until validated.

## Slide 21 - Scenario: an operator must control the system safely.

Admin is part of the product architecture, but only capability-level details are public-safe.

## Slide 22 - Risk and approvals protect the platform loop.

Public collateral can describe governance without exposing implementation details.

## Slide 23 - Money-state flow: wallet to product to settlement.

Wallet,Policy,Order,Settlement,History

## Slide 24 - Growth flow: account state to community retention.

Profile,VIP,Invite,Revenue,Universe

## Slide 25 - Current, In Progress and Planned must stay separate.

Current,In Progress,Planned,Approval

## Slide 26 - Customer-facing version: show the product journey.

Customer copy starts with what users see and how they move.

## Slide 27 - Partner-facing version: show the operating system.

Partner copy adds architecture and go-to-market logic without exposing private internals.

## Slide 28 - Public safety boundary for every product chapter.

Trust is built by explaining enough, and withholding what should remain private.

## Slide 29 - What replaces PRODUCT-BOOK-037?

The primary asset now describes the real platform architecture.

## Slide 30 - Next step: use this book as the product source.

One product architecture now drives the public story.
